RED WINE CHEESE FONDUE

"A special treat for those you hold dear."
- Tamara Camacho

Cheese fondue is often considered and expensive and luxurious treat, a meal served at specialty restaurants only. However, during the winter months, When we find ourselves most in need of warm food, leaving the home is a struggle at best, impossible at worst. This simple recipe, modified from the standard white wine fondue, is easy to make from home and packs and extra punch of flavor. Fast and tasty, fondue can be combined with plenty of breads and fruits, making each serving an experiment. After making this traditional swiss recipe a few times, you'll never want to go outside for fondue ever again.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup red cooking wine
  • ½ lb swiss cheese
  • ½ lb gruyere cheese
  • 3 tablespoons conrnstarch
  • ¼ teaspon salt
  • ¼ teaspon ground nutmeg
  • 1 (1 lb) loaf of gluten-free bread
  • 1 cup of apples and/or grapes (not absolutely necessary)

Gluten-free
Serves 4
Prep time: 10 mins
Cooking time: 15 mins

Directions

  1. Pour the red wine into fondue pot and wait for it to simmer.
  2. Cut both cheeses into blocks of approximately ½ inch each.
  3. Once the wine simmers, add swiss cheese and gruyere cheese, ¼ lb at a time. Stir in each addition thoroughly melted.
  4. When all the cheese has melted, mix cornstarch with wine in separate container. Add to cheese and wine.
  5. Wait for the the mixture to combine fully. Cut the loaf of gluten-free bread (and apples, if you're adding those) into 1 inch pieces while you wait.
  6. Stir in salt and nutmeg, and serve with bread and/or fruit.
